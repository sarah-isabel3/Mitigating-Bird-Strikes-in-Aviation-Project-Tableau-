## Overview

Bird strikes in aviation pose safety, operational, and financial risks for airlines and airports worldwide. This Tableau project analyzes a comprehensive bird strike dataset, using interactive dashboards and visual storytelling to:
- Identify high-risk aircraft, airports, flight phases, and wildlife species
- Quantify operations disruption and costs
- Evaluate the effectiveness of pilot warnings and crew training
- Recommend strategies for prevention, conservation, and cost reduction

## Features

- **Data Preprocessing & Cleaning:** Rigorous data wrangling ensures analysis accuracy and dashboard performance.
- **Multi-Dashboard Solution:** Includes dashboards for:
    - Safety Improvement & Wildlife Risk Patterns
    - Bird Strike Reporting & Compliance
    - Wildlife Conservation & Human Safety Impact
    - Operational Efficiency
    - Cost Reduction Analysis
    - Bird Strike Risk Management, Training & Communication
- **Storytelling Approach:** Each dashboard is designed to clarify specific risk areas with business-ready narratives and recommendations.
- **Predictive Insights:** Uses historical patterns to advise scheduling and mitigation.

## Business Value

- **Actionable recommendations:** Targeted risk prevention at specific airports, seasons, and for certain aircraft types/species.
- **Operational insights:** Optimize flight scheduling and pilot briefings for efficiency and safety.
- **Financial analysis:** Identify cost drivers, frequency, and severity of events for smarter budgeting.
- **Training impact:** Prove the effectiveness of pilot warnings and data-driven crew training programs.

## Getting Started

1. **Data:** Download or use the provided cleaned dataset and data dictionary for field definitions.
2. **Dashboards:** Open the Tableau `.twbx` files in Tableau Desktop or Tableau Public. Filter/interact for scenario exploration.
3. **Presentation:** Refer to the included project presentation (`.pptx` or `.pdf`) for executive summaries and recommendations.
4. **Interpretations:** Each dashboard folder contains a readme/summary file highlighting interpretations and suggested actions.
